How this study works

  1. Complete this form in full. Fields marked * are required.
  2. You will be guided through a series of tasks. Shape tracing tasks come first, followed by writing tasks.
  3. Some tasks are repeated multiple times — a repetition counter will show your progress within each task.
  4. Each repetition has its own canvas. Write or trace naturally, at your normal pace.
  5. At the end of each repetition tap Save & Continue. Your data will be sent securely to the server.
  6. We strongly prefer Apple Pencil or stylus input, as these provide pressure and tilt data. Finger, mouse, and trackpad input are also accepted.
  7. Thank you sincerely for your participation. Your contribution directly supports research into handwriting dynamics and neurological condition detection.

Informed Consent & Data Use

Participant Consent Statement

By participating in this study, you acknowledge and agree to the following:

1. Nature of data collected. This study collects digitised handwriting data, including the spatial coordinates, timing, pressure, and tilt of each pen stroke, together with the demographic and clinical information provided on this form. No audio, video, or personally identifying information (such as name, address, or contact details) is collected.

2. Purpose of research. The data will be used to investigate characteristics of handwriting motion, including the effects of neurological conditions such as Parkinson's disease on pen-stroke dynamics. Results may be used to develop or evaluate computational models of handwriting analysis.

3. Publication and sharing. Anonymised data, including stroke data and de-identified demographic responses, may be included in academic publications, conference presentations, or publicly released research datasets. No individual participant will be identifiable in any published output.

4. Voluntary participation. Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time before submitting your data by closing this page. Once submitted, it may not be possible to identify and remove your individual contribution.

5. Data storage. Data will be stored securely and accessed only by the research team. It will not be sold or shared with third parties for commercial purposes.

Please complete all required fields and tick both consent boxes before continuing.
